Differentiation: Composite, Implicit, and Inverse Functions

3.1 The Chain Rule

  • Used to differentiate composite functions.
  • If \( y = f(g(x)) \), then \( y' = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x) \).
  • Example: If \( y = (3x^2 + 2x + 1)^5 \), let \( u = 3x^2 + 2x + 1 \):
    • \( y = u^5 \rightarrow y' = 5u^4 \cdot \frac{du}{dx} \)
    • \( \frac{du}{dx} = 6x + 2 \)
    • Thus, \( y' = 5(3x^2 + 2x + 1)^4 \cdot (6x + 2) \)

3.2 Implicit Differentiation

  • Used when functions are not explicitly solved for one variable in terms of another.
  • Differentiate both sides of the equation with respect to \( x \) using the Chain Rule.
  • Example: If \( x^2 + y^2 = 1 \), differentiate each term:
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(x^2) + \frac{d}{dx}(y^2) = \frac{d}{dx}(1) \)
    • \( 2x + 2y \frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \rightarrow \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{x}{y} \)

3.3 Differentiating Inverse Functions

  • If \( y = f^{-1}(x) \), then \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(x))} \).
  • Example: For \( y = \ln(x) \) (the inverse function of \( e^x \)):
    • \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{e^{\ln(x)}} = \frac{1}{x} \)

3.4 Inverse Trigonometric Functions

  • Derivatives of inverse trig functions:
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\sin^{-1}(x)) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\cos^{-1}(x)) = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\tan^{-1}(x)) = \frac{1}{1+x^2} \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\cot^{-1}(x)) = -\frac{1}{1+x^2} \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\sec^{-1}(x)) = \frac{1}{|x|\sqrt{x^2-1}} \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\csc^{-1}(x)) = -\frac{1}{|x|\sqrt{x^2-1}} \)

3.5 General Properties of Logarithmic and Exponential Functions

  • Differentiation rules for exponential and logarithmic functions:
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(e^x) = e^x \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(a^x) = a^x \ln(a) \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\ln(x)) = \frac{1}{x} \)
    • \( \frac{d}{dx}(\log_a(x)) = \frac{1}{x \ln(a)} \)
  • Logarithmic differentiation:
    • Useful for functions of the form \( y = f(x)^{g(x)} \).
    • Take the natural logarithm of both sides and then differentiate.
  • Example: Differentiate \( y = x^x \):
    • \( \ln(y) = x \ln(x) \)
    • \( \frac{1}{y} \frac{dy}{dx} = \ln(x) + 1 \)
    • So, \( \frac{dy}{dx} = y (\ln(x) + 1) = x^x (\ln(x) + 1) \)

3.6 Derivatives of Functions Defined Parametrically

  • Used for parametric equations where \( x \) and \( y \) are functions of a parameter \( t \).
  • If \( x = f(t) \) and \( y = g(t) \), then \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\frac{dy}{dt}}{\frac{dx}{dt}} \).
  • Second derivative: \( \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right) = \frac{\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)}{\frac{dx}{dt}} \).
  • Example: Given \( x = t^2 \) and \( y = t^3 \):
    • \( \frac{dx}{dt} = 2t \) and \( \frac{dy}{dt} = 3t^2 \)
    • \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{3t^2}{2t} = \frac{3t}{2} \)
