AP Calculus BC - Unit 7: Differential Equations

7.1 Modeling Situations with Differential Equations

Understanding how to represent real-world scenarios using differential equations. A differential equation expresses a relationship involving rates of change and the functions they affect.

Example:

Consider a population growing at a rate proportional to its size. This can be modeled by the differential equation \( \frac{dP}{dt} = kP \).

Solution: The solution to this differential equation is \( P(t) = P_0 e^{kt} \), where \( P_0 \) is the initial population and \( k \) is the growth constant.

7.2 Verifying Solutions for Differential Equations

You’ll need to determine whether a given function satisfies a given differential equation.

Example:

Verify that \( y = e^{4x} \) is a solution to the differential equation \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 4y \).

Solution: Compute \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 4e^{4x} \). Since \( 4e^{4x} = 4y \), the function \( y = e^{4x} \) satisfies the differential equation.

7.3 Sketching Slope Fields

A slope field is a graphical representation of a differential equation, showing the direction of the tangent lines at various points.

Example:

Sketch a slope field for \( \frac{dy}{dx} = x - y \).

Solution: Calculate the slopes at points on a grid, e.g., at (1,1), \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 1-1 = 0 \); at (0,0), \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 0-0 = 0 \); at (2,1), \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 2-1 = 1 \), etc. Draw small line segments with these slopes at the respective points.

7.4 Finding General Solutions using Separation of Variables

Solve differential equations by separating variables and integrating each side.

Example:

Solve \( \frac{dy}{dx} = y \cdot \cos(x) \).

Solution: Separate variables: \( \frac{dy}{y} = \cos(x) \, dx \). Integrate both sides: \( \ln|y| = \sin(x) + C \). Solve for y: \( y = Ce^{\sin(x)} \).

7.5 Finding Particular Solutions using Initial Conditions

Use an initial condition to find the particular solution for a differential equation.

Example:

Solve \( \frac{dy}{dx} = xy \) with initial condition \( y(0) = 3 \).

Solution: Separate: \( \frac{dy}{y} = x \, dx \). Integrate: \( \ln|y| = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \). Solve for y: \( y = Ce^{\frac{x^2}{2}} \). Use initial condition: \( 3 = Ce^0 \rightarrow C = 3 \). Therefore, \( y = 3e^{\frac{x^2}{2}} \).

7.6 Exponential Growth and Decay

Modeling scenarios where a quantity grows or decays at a rate proportional to its size.

Example:

P(t) = P0ekt models population growth.

Solution: For a population of 100 growing at 5% per year: \( P(t) = 100e^{0.05t} \).

7.7 Logistic Growth Models

Modeling scenarios with a carrying capacity, where growth slows as population size approaches the carrying capacity.

Example:

The logistic model is \( P(t) = \frac{K}{1 + \left( \frac{K - P_0}{P_0} \right)e^{-rt}} \).

Solution: For a population with carrying capacity 500, initial population 10, and growth rate 0.1: \( P(t) = \frac{500}{1 + 49e^{-0.1t}} \).

7.8 Euler’s Method

A numerical approach to solving differential equations.

Example:

Using Euler’s Method with step size 0.1 to approximate y(0.5) given \( y' = y + x \) and y(0) = 1.

Solution: Step 1: \( y_1 = y_0 + f(x_0, y_0)\Delta x = 1 + (1+0) \cdot 0.1 = 1.1 \). Continue stepping forward to approximate y(0.5).

7.9 Analytical, Numerical, and Graphical Approaches

Solving differential equations using various methods: exact solutions, approximations, and visualizations.

Example:

Compare exact, Euler, and slope field methods on \( \frac{dy}{dx} = x + y \) with y(0)=1.

Solution: Exact: \( y = Ce^x - x - 1 \). Euler and slope field as described in respective sections.
