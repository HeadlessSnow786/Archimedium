Unit 8: Applications of Integration

8.1: Finding the Average Value of a Function on an Interval

The average value of a continuous function f(x) over the interval [a, b] is given by:

$$\text{Average Value} = \frac{1}{b-a} \int_a^b f(x) \, dx$$

Example: Find the average value of \(f(x) = x^2\) on the interval [1, 3].

$$\text{Average Value} = \frac{1}{3-1} \int_1^3 x^2 \, dx$$
$$=\frac{1}{2} \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_1^3$$
$$=\frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{27}{3} - \frac{1}{3} \right)$$
$$=\frac{1}{2} \left( 9 - \frac{1}{3} \right)$$
$$=\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{26}{3}$$
$$=\frac{13}{3}$$

8.2: Using Definite Integrals to Find the Area between Curves

The area between two curves, \( y = f(x) \) and \( y = g(x) \), from \( x=a \) to \( x=b \) is:

$$\text{Area} = \int_a^b (f(x) - g(x)) \, dx$$

Example: Find the area between \( y = x^2 \) and \( y = x+2 \) from \( x=0 \) to \( x=2 \).

$$\text{Area} = \int_0^2 ((x+2) - x^2) \, dx$$
$$= \int_0^2 (x+2 - x^2) \, dx$$
$$= \left[ \frac{x^2}{2} + 2x - \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_0^2$$
$$= \left( \frac{4}{2} + 4 - \frac{8}{3} \right) - \left( 0 + 0 - 0 \right)$$
$$= 2 + 4 - \frac{8}{3}$$
$$= 6 - \frac{8}{3}$$
$$= \frac{18}{3} - \frac{8}{3}$$
$$= \frac{10}{3}$$

8.3: Volume with Cross Sections: Disk Method

To find the volume of a solid of revolution using the disk method, we use:

$$V = \pi \int_a^b [f(x)]^2 \, dx$$

Example: Find the volume of the solid formed by revolving \( y = \sqrt{x} \) around the x-axis from \( x=0 \) to \( x=1 \).

$$V = \pi \int_0^1 (\sqrt{x})^2 \, dx$$
$$= \pi \int_0^1 x \, dx$$
$$= \pi \left[ \frac{x^2}{2} \right]_0^1$$
$$= \pi \left( \frac{1}{2} - 0 \right)$$
$$= \frac{\pi}{2}$$

8.4: Volume with Cross Sections: Washer Method

To find the volume of a solid of revolution using the washer method, we use:

$$V = \pi \int_a^b ([R(x)]^2 - [r(x)]^2) \, dx$$

where \( R(x) \) is the outer radius and \( r(x) \) is the inner radius.

Example: Find the volume of the solid formed by revolving \( y = x+1 \) and \( y = x \) around the x-axis from \( x=0 \) to \( x=1 \).

$$V = \pi \int_0^1 ((x+1)^2 - x^2) \, dx$$
$$= \pi \int_0^1 (x^2 + 2x + 1 - x^2) \, dx$$
$$= \pi \int_0^1 (2x + 1) \, dx$$
$$= \pi \left[ x^2 + x \right]_0^1$$
$$= \pi \left( 1 + 1 - 0 - 0 \right)$$
$$= 2\pi$$

8.5: Volumes of Revolution: Cylindrical Shells

To find the volume of a solid of revolution using cylindrical shells, we use:

$$V = 2\pi \int_a^b x \cdot f(x) \, dx$$

Example: Find the volume of the solid formed by revolving \( y = x^2 \) around the y-axis from \( x=0 \) to \( x=1 \).

$$V = 2\pi \int_0^1 x \cdot x^2 \, dx$$
$$= 2\pi \int_0^1 x^3 \, dx$$
$$= 2\pi \left[ \frac{x^4}{4} \right]_0^1$$
$$= 2\pi \left( \frac{1}{4} - 0 \right)$$
$$= \frac{\pi}{2}$$

8.6: Arc Length of a Curve and Surface Area

The arc length of a function \( y = f(x) \) from \( x=a \) to \( x=b \) is:

$$L = \int_a^b \sqrt{1 + \left( \frac{dy}{dx} \right)^2} \, dx$$

Example: Find the arc length of \( y = x^2 \) from \( x=0 \) to \( x=1 \).

$$L = \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + (2x)^2} \, dx$$
$$= \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + 4x^2} \, dx$$
We can solve this using a trigonometric substitution or a tables method. This specific integral is beyond basic integral techniques and would typically be solved numerically.

8.7: Applications from Physics, Economics, and Statistics

Integration can be used in various practical applications:

  • Physics: For finding displacement, velocity, work, center of mass, etc.
  • Economics: For finding consumer and producer surplus.
  • Statistics: For determining probabilities using probability density functions.

Example in Physics: Work

Find the work done by a force \( F(x) = x \) from \( x=0 \) to \( x=2 \).

$$W = \int_0^2 F(x) \, dx$$
$$= \int_0^2 x \, dx$$
$$= \left[ \frac{x^2}{2} \right]_0^2$$
$$= \left( \frac{4}{2} - 0 \right)$$
$$= 2 \, \text{Joules}$$

Example in Economics: Consumer Surplus

If the demand function is \( p = 10 - x \) and the equilibrium price is \( p = 2 \), find the consumer surplus.

$$\text{Consumer Surplus} = \int_0^8 (10 - x - 2) \, dx$$
$$= \int_0^8 (8 - x) \, dx$$
$$= \left[ 8x - \frac{x^2}{2} \right]_0^8$$
$$= \left( 64 - 32 - 0 \right)$$
$$= 32 \, \text{units of currency}$$
